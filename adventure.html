Project Overview

An adventure-themed level design project inspired by Indiana Jones and Tomb Raider, focusing on intuitive navigation, environmental storytelling, and team collaboration. I designed a section of a larger connected level while maintaining consistency with the overall adventure theme.

As part of a team of designers, I created puzzle systems, managed asset integration from Epic Games Marketplace, and used Perforce for source control collaboration. The project emphasized player-driven exploration through landmarks and visual cues.

My Contributions

  • Designed gate and valve puzzle systems optimizing player progression, flow, and navigation clarity
  • Implemented Blueprint-based mechanics using environmental storytelling, landmarks, and leading lines
  • Utilized Perforce source control for team collaboration and seamless asset integration
  • Managed asset sourcing and integration from Epic Games Marketplace while maintaining theme consistency
  • Conducted peer playtesting to gather feedback and refine level design decisions
  • Created blockout meshes to establish spatial layout before asset placement
  • Collaborated with team members to ensure smooth transitions between level sections

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge 1: Visual Consistency

Player Feedback: "The different sections of the level feel disconnected" and "The visual style doesn't match between areas."

The Solution: After completing the blockmesh phase, I returned to our team's mood board to ensure my asset selections aligned with the Indiana Jones and Tomb Raider-inspired adventure theme. By carefully sourcing free assets from Epic Games Marketplace that matched our established aesthetic, I maintained thematic consistency while adding unique details to my section. This methodical approach to asset curation ensured cohesive visual storytelling across the entire level.

Challenge 2: Gameplay Depth

Player Feedback: "The platforming is fun but I want more reasons to explore" and "It feels like I'm just going from point A to point B."

The Solution: While playtesting revealed players enjoyed the platforming challenges, I recognized opportunities to deepen engagement through Blueprint implementation. In retrospect, I could have enhanced the experience by introducing vertical exploration elements and collectible systems tied to gate progression. This taught me to think holistically about how technical systems can elevate level design without expanding scope unnecessarily.

Challenge 3: Team Communication

Team Feedback: "We missed another scheduled meeting" and "I'm not sure what everyone else is working on."

The Solution: When scheduled team meetings didn't happen as planned, it created gaps in collaborative feedback and morale. I learned that while I can't control others' dedication, I can control my own professionalism and output quality. This experience reinforced the importance of establishing clear communication protocols, respecting teammates' time, and seeking peer feedback proactively. Moving forward, I've made it a priority to document decisions and maintain open channels for asynchronous collaboration.

What I Learned

The transition from blockmesh to final asset placement taught me how spatial layout directly influences player psychology and navigation. This iterative design process deepened my appreciation for testing early and often before committing to detailed work.

Time management emerged as crucial throughout this project. By treating development like a professional studio environment with full workdays, I developed sustainable workflows balancing creativity with productivity. This disciplined approach improved my output quality and helped overcome creative anxieties about designing a complete level section.

Working with Perforce and managing team integration taught me that technical tools are only as effective as the communication supporting them. The challenges with team coordination became valuable lessons in professional resilience—understanding that collaborative success requires both technical competence and interpersonal awareness.

Most importantly, this project solidified my passion for level design as a career path. Creating immersive, navigable spaces that tell stories through environmental design confirmed this is where I want to focus my professional growth, combining my creative and technical strengths in meaningful ways.
