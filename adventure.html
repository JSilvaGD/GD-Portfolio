Project Overview

An adventure-themed level design project inspired by Indiana Jones and Tomb Raider, focusing on creating intuitive navigation, environmental storytelling, and cohesive team collaboration. This project challenged me to design a section of a larger connected level while maintaining consistency with the overall adventure theme.

As part of a team of designers, I was responsible for creating puzzle systems, managing asset integration from Epic Games Marketplace, and using source control (Perforce) for seamless team collaboration. The project emphasized player-driven exploration through clever use of landmarks and visual cues.

My Contributions

  • Designed gate and valve puzzle systems optimizing player progression, flow, and navigation clarity
  • Implemented Blueprint-based mechanics using environmental storytelling, landmarks, and leading lines
  • Utilized Perforce source control for team collaboration and seamless asset integration
  • Managed asset sourcing and integration from Epic Games Marketplace while maintaining theme consistency

  • Collaborated with team members to ensure smooth transitions between level sections

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge: Maintaining visual consistency across different team members' level sections while staying within the project scope.

Solution: After completing the blockmesh phase, I returned to our team's mood board to ensure my asset selections aligned with the Indiana Jones and Tomb Raider-inspired adventure theme. By carefully sourcing free assets from Epic Games Marketplace that matched our established aesthetic, I maintained thematic consistency while adding unique details to my section. This methodical approach to asset curation ensured cohesive visual storytelling across the entire level.

Challenge: Balancing gameplay complexity with the project's scope and timeline.

Solution: While playtesting revealed that players enjoyed the platforming challenges, I recognized opportunities to deepen the experience through Blueprint implementation. In retrospect, I could have enhanced player engagement by introducing vertical exploration elements and collectible systems tied to gate progression. This taught me to think more holistically about how technical systems can elevate level design without expanding scope unnecessarily.

Challenge: Navigating inconsistent team communication and varying commitment levels.

Solution: When scheduled team meetings didn't happen as planned, it created gaps in collaborative feedback and morale. I learned that while I can't control others' dedication, I can control my own professionalism and output quality. This experience reinforced the importance of establishing clear communication protocols, respecting teammates' time, and seeking peer feedback proactively. Moving forward, I've made it a priority to document decisions and maintain open channels for asynchronous collaboration.

What I Learned

This project was transformative in my understanding of level design fundamentals and collaborative development. The transition from blockmesh to final asset placement taught me how spatial layout directly influences player psychology and navigation. Seeing the level evolve from simple blocks to a fully realized adventure environment deepened my appreciation for iterative design processes.

Time management emerged as a crucial skill throughout this project. By committing to full workdays and treating the project like a professional studio environment, I developed sustainable workflows that balanced creativity with productivity. This disciplined approach not only improved my output quality but also helped me overcome initial creative anxieties about designing a complete level section.

Working with Perforce and managing team integration taught me that technical tools are only as effective as the communication supporting them. The challenges with team coordination became valuable lessons in professional resilience—understanding that collaborative success requires both technical competence and interpersonal awareness. These experiences have shaped how I approach team dynamics in subsequent projects.

Perhaps most importantly, this project solidified my passion for level design as a career path. The challenge of creating immersive, navigable spaces that tell stories through environmental design confirmed this is where I want to focus my professional growth. Every aspect—from asset placement to player flow optimization—reinforced that level design combines my creative and technical strengths in meaningful ways.
